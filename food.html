Healthy food

    Basic facts:

  • A healthy diet provides protection from malnutrition in all its forms, as well as from non-communicable diseases (NCDs), including diabetes, heart disease, stroke and cancer. Unhealthy diet and lack of physical activity are the main health risks worldwide.

  • The practice of healthy eating is formed in the early stages of life ― breastfeeding promotes healthy growth and improves cognitive development and can have a beneficial effect on health in the long term, for example, reduces the likelihood of overweight or obesity and the development of NCDs later in life.

  • Energy consumption (calories) should be balanced with its consumption. In order to avoid unhealthy weight gain, the total fat intake should not exceed 30% of the total energy consumed. Saturated fats should be less than 10%, and trans fats – less than 1% of the total energy consumed, and when consuming fats, it is necessary to replace saturated fats and trans fats with unsaturated fats and strive to exclude trans fats from the diet of industrial production.

  • Reducing the consumption of free sugars to less than 10% of the total energy consumed is part of a healthy diet, and reducing their consumption to less than 5% presumably provides additional health benefits.

  • Salt intake of less than 5 g per day (equivalent to sodium intake of less than 2 g per day) promotes the prevention of hypertension and reduces the risk of heart disease and stroke among the adult population.

  • WHO member States have put forward a goal to reduce global salt consumption by 30% by 2025, as well as to stop the increase in the number of cases of diabetes and obesity in adults and adolescents and overweight in children by 2025.

    • A healthy diet throughout life contributes to the prevention of malnutrition in all its forms, as well as a number of non-communicable diseases (NCDs) and health disorders. At the same time, the growth of processed food production, rapid urbanization and changing lifestyles have led to a shift in nutrition patterns. Nowadays, people consume more foods high in calories, fats, free sugars and salt/sodium, and many people don't consume enough fruits, vegetables and other types of fiber, such as whole grains.

    The exact composition of a varied, balanced and healthy diet depends on individual characteristics (such as age, gender, lifestyle and degree of physical activity), cultural context, available local products and dietary customs. However, the basic principles of a healthy diet remain the same.
